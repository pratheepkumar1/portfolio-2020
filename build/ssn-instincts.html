Overview

SSN Instincts, since its inception in 2006, is one of the largest campus cultural fests in South India, attracting crowds of well over 10,000 students from over 150 institutions of higher learning. Each year, a unique theme will be followed for defining fest based on which tagline, event name, the decoration of the campus and much more are decided. This year (2017), Team Instincts come up with the theme “Carnival” and the tagline “Ride the Roller Coaster”. We need to develop a website to showcase the events, schedule and other details about the cultural fest.

Duration Feb 2017 - Mar 2017 (15 Days)

Gathering
The Requirements

Since every year, our college cultural website keeps on changing, we looked at how previous year design was made and how other top colleges’ cultural websites in our demography were made. We found that most of them used some random template designs which lack liveliness. Then we understood that should deliver a real-time cultural fest experience in our website since it is the most important point of contact to know about our cultural fest.

Searching for
The Mimicry

For providing indistinguishable experience of exploring a carnival kind of experience in our website, we took the surface mimicry from the actual carnival arena and our college environment. We used various elements from the carnivals and our college in building components for the website. Our main idea was to create an environment which suits the theme. At the same time, it should trigger the user to explore more.

FEB 2018
Sketching out
Some Rough Concepts

Since we have only limited time duration left for the deployment of the website, we started sketching our ideas and figured out what we are actually trying to acheive. We picked few of the sketches and started iterating it better. At each iteration we got some new thing that improved the overall experience and visualization of the information. Following are few iterations that made great impacts to the final design.

Carnival Environment

We understood horizontal scroll might help us to create an environment that would make the user move around as if they were playing a 2D game. We added few rides in the carnival to represent each of the sections in our website

Additional College Buildings as Elements

Finally we added few of building and architecture to spice up the visual identity that matches our college’s actual iconic architectures. Such that when the user visit our college, they will be able relate with what they have see in the website

Navigation: Riding a Roller Coaster

Then we thought we could add roller coaster for the movement of the user across the website. Such that scrolling or using arrow keys would essentially move the user in the respective direction

FEB 2018
Building
The Components

We created the components for the environment first in a way that will be repeated across wherever required. Based on the sketches, we created the buildings and games that match the particular heading. On the whole, we ensured that we every component we designed are similar in shape and similar in the pattern. We tried to follow two tones in style for all the components that we have built. We chose the color palette that is prominently used in a carnival setup. And also the fonts that we have chosen set the mood of a carnival.

FEB 2018
Designing
The Web Interface

Taking the rough concept into account, we started building the single landing page with all the necessarry components designed. Because of the horizontal layout of the landing page, due to time constraints we couldn’t focus much on responsiveness on smaller devices. However, we are able to provide the horizontal layout more intuitive on large screen devices. The navigation bar is fixed at the top which provides navigation to all the key sections in the landing page. For each displaying the information of individual section, we displayed their revalant information in a popup modal.
